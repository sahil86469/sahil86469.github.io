Project 1

Path-Planning for UGV

The robot's static programming involves defining movement through preset waypoints, complemented by obstacle detection via 3D point cloud sensors and camera-based region of interest detection. Two methods are employed: Move Base package utilizing DWA and trajectory planning, and base programming employing A* with local and global maps. Simulations are conducted in Gazebo and RVIZ for initial testing, with sensor data collection and final testing executed on UGV with significant modifications. Additionally, airport tug design and integration with PLANSYS2 in ROS 2 are undertaken for comprehensive system development.

Project 2

Knee rehablitation robot

This project focuses on developing an innovative knee rehabilitation robotic system driven by AI, offering personalized therapy for patients. By combining X-rays and machine learning, early detection and prediction of knee issues, such as osteoarthritis severity, are enabled. The system includes a mechanism to aid rehabilitation by providing feedback on user movements, facilitating faster recovery and reducing injury risks. This non-invasive, affordable solution has the potential to revolutionize knee problem detection and treatment, enhancing the lives of affected individuals.

Project 3

Borewell robot

The Borwell Rescue System integrates advanced technologies like AI, real-time data analytics, and modern communication protocols to facilitate swift and effective responses to emergencies involving borewells. By employing predictive modeling, it optimizes resource allocation and decision-making, reducing casualties and accelerating reaction times. The system's adaptability allows it to address various emergency scenarios, from accidents to natural disasters, customizing responses as needed. A mechanical apparatus controlled by Arduino enables efficient extraction of trapped children from borewells, mitigating risks associated with traditional rescue methods.

Project 4

Swarm robotics

Homogeneous swarm robots play a crucial role in cooperative problem-solving by sharing information, enabling swift and efficient task completion compared to individual robots. This is especially vital in search and rescue missions where risks are involved. Through wireless intercommunication, Turtlebot3 robots coordinate effectively, mapping environments and strategizing systematic rescues. Challenges such as collisions and uncertainty regarding other robots' intentions necessitate coordination efforts. The project utilizes Webot simulation to program and simulate intercommunication among Turtlebot3 swarm robots, addressing these challenges and enhancing rescue mission efficiency.
